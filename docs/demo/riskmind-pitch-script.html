Q&A — Ready Answers
Keep deck on Slide 11 during Q&A
"How is this different from our existing analytics/claims system?"
Your claims system tells you what happened. RiskMind tells you what to do about it — and backs it with your own guidelines, evidence, and confidence scoring. Dashboard vs. co-pilot.
"What if the AI gives a wrong recommendation?"
Two safeguards. First, the AI never auto-decides — the underwriter makes the final call. Second, the Glass Box shows exactly what data and guidelines led to the recommendation. If something looks wrong, the underwriter sees why in 5 seconds. Plus, confidence scoring below 50% triggers clarification — it won't guess.
"What about data security?"
Your data stays in your environment. Read-only database access. LLM calls use AWS Bedrock within your VPC — no data leaves your perimeter. We can deploy fully on-premise if needed.
"Our underwriters aren't tech-savvy."
It's a chat interface. If they can type a question, they can use RiskMind. 30-minute onboarding. The Workbench gives a familiar dashboard for daily tasks.
"How long to implement?"
Pilot: 2-3 days to connect your database, ingest your underwriting manual, configure roles. Full production: 2-4 weeks including tuning and integration testing.
"What does it cost?"
Per seat pricing, starting at $X per underwriter per month. For a pilot, we typically do a fixed-fee 30-day engagement. Happy to send a proposal.
"We already have Guidewire / Duck Creek / Majesco."
RiskMind doesn't replace your policy admin system — it sits on top. Think of it as the intelligence layer. It reads from Guidewire but gives your underwriter conversational AI, guideline citations, and evidence — things your PAS was never built to do.
"What LLM do you use? Can we use our own?"
LangChain abstraction layer — swap models without code changes. We support Claude, Bedrock, Gemini, OpenAI. If you have a preferred provider or internal model, we plug it in.
