The Solution — RiskMind
4:00 — 3 min
RiskMind is a Glass Box AI. Not a black box. [pause]

The idea is simple: your underwriter asks a question in plain English — "Why is this construction policy high risk?" — and gets back an instant analysis with four things no other tool gives you:
  • The numbers — KPIs computed deterministically from your actual data. Loss ratio, claim frequency, severity trends. AI never touches the database. Deterministic code does all the math.
  • The evidence — The actual claim photos, PDFs, inspection reports that support the analysis. Not just text. Visual evidence.
  • The guidelines — Specific section codes from your underwriting manual cited with exact quotes. "Section 4.1 says loss ratio above 80% triggers mandatory surcharge."
  • The confidence — A transparent score showing how confident the AI is and why. If it's unsure, it asks for clarification instead of guessing.
AI recommends. The underwriter decides. Glass Box proves why.

That's the model. The AI does the data hunting, the cross-referencing, the memo drafting. The underwriter does what only a human can do: exercise judgment and make the call. [pause]

Let me explain why the Glass Box architecture is the real differentiator here, and why it matters for your business. [pause]

In most AI tools, the AI writes queries, crunches numbers, and gives you results. The problem is — if the AI makes a mistake in the query, you'd never know. The numbers look plausible. They show up in a memo. Someone signs it. [pause]

In RiskMind, the AI never touches the database. All the math — loss ratios, claim severity, frequency analysis, risk scoring — that's all deterministic code. Pre-computed. Verified. The AI's job is only to explain what the code already calculated. It can't hallucinate a loss ratio because it didn't compute it.

The Glass Box has four layers of proof on every single response:

Layer one — Data Lineage: Which tables were queried. "This answer used policies, claims, and decisions tables." Full provenance.

Layer four — Confidence Score: 82% confidence with transparent reason codes. If the AI isn't sure — below 50% — it doesn't guess. It asks for clarification. That alone prevents 90% of hallucination problems.

Objection Handling & FAQ
Q&A prep
"How is this different from what we get with our existing claims analytics?"
Claims analytics tells you what happened. RiskMind tells you what to do about it — and backs it up with your own guidelines, evidence, and confidence scoring. It's the difference between a dashboard and a co-pilot.
"What if the AI gives a wrong recommendation?"
Two safeguards. First, the AI never auto-decides — the underwriter always makes the final call. Second, the Glass Box shows exactly what data and guidelines led to the recommendation. If something looks off, the underwriter can see why in 5 seconds and override it. Plus, our confidence scoring — if the AI is below 50% certain, it won't even recommend. It'll ask for clarification instead.
"We're worried about data security. Where does our data go?"
Your data stays in your environment. RiskMind connects to your database with read-only access — we never write to it, never copy it externally. The AI models can run on AWS Bedrock within your VPC, or we can deploy fully on-premise. No data leaves your perimeter.
"Our underwriters are not tech-savvy. Will they actually use this?"
It's a chat interface. If they can type a question in English, they can use RiskMind. No SQL, no query builders, no training on a new dashboard tool. We've designed it so a 30-minute onboarding session is all they need. And the Workbench gives them a familiar dashboard view for daily tasks.
"How long does implementation take?"
For a pilot: 2-3 days to connect your database, ingest your underwriting manual, and configure user roles. Full production deployment is typically 2-4 weeks including custom tuning and integration testing.
"Can it handle our specific lines of business?"
Currently optimized for commercial property and casualty. The guideline RAG system is line-agnostic — we index whatever underwriting manuals you use. So specialty lines, professional liability, marine — all work. The analytics and risk scoring adapt to whatever schema your data uses.
"What about SOC 2 / compliance certifications?"
We're building toward SOC 2 Type II. For the pilot phase, we deploy within your AWS environment using Bedrock, so your existing compliance posture covers the AI layer. No external API calls — everything stays inside your VPC.
"We already have Guidewire / Duck Creek / Majesco. Why add another tool?"
RiskMind doesn't replace your policy admin system — it sits on top of it. Think of it as the intelligence layer. It reads from Guidewire, but gives your underwriter a conversational interface with AI-powered analysis, guideline citations, and evidence — things your PAS was never designed to do.
